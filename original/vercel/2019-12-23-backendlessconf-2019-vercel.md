Dec 23, 2019

2019 has been an incredible year for Vercel. We announced [zero-config](https://vercel.com/blog/zero-config), launched a new [integrations platform](https://vercel.com/blog/zeit-now-integrations-platform), and even hosted a successful [hackathon](https://vercel.com/blog/zeit-hackathon-winners).

To end the year on a memorable note, we held our **first-ever remote conference:** [**backendlessConf\_**](https://backendlessconf.com/).

### The Stats

This first edition of backendlessConf\_ received a lot of interest from the developer community.

- **3,888** registrations from every corner of the world

- **1,500+** live attendees throughout various points in the event

- **1,374** participants on the dedicated Slack


## The backendlessConf\_ Experience

We believe it's the new friendships and connections that make any great conference memorable. To facilitate connections in a remote setting, we set up a [**dedicated Slack**](https://backendlessconf.com/slack) for backendlessConf\_.

We also created a `/gif` command to help participants record themselves into a GIF and add a fun and personal element to their introductions.

It was absolutely heartwarming to see developers from every corner of the world join us!

On the day of the conference, [**backendlessconf.com**](https://backendlessconf.com/) transformed itself to include a conference video stream, as well as a **live feed** of all activity from Slack and Twitter — messages, our assortment of emoji, even GIFs!

![Live feed of chat activity next to the stream.](images/backendlessconf-2019-vercel/img_001.jpg)Live feed of chat activity next to the stream.

## The Sessions

A total of **16 international speakers**, representing various aspects of the frontend and JAMstack joined us live for backendlessConf\_. They brought with them a diverse range of content, and patiently answered questions from all the participants.


[Guillermo](https://twitter.com/rauchg) ( [Vercel](https://vercel.com/)), [Ahmad](https://twitter.com/MrAhmadAwais) ( [Cloudinary](https://cloudinary.rocks/cejbz)), and [Sadek](https://twitter.com/prismicio) ( [Prismic](https://prismic.io/)) did the first set of sessions, elaborating on experiences with the JAMstack, and demonstrating the state of the art in building and editing modern websites.


[Maya](https://twitter.com/MayaShavin) ( [Cloudinary](https://cloudinary.rocks/cejbz)), [Eva](https://twitter.com/evaieridou) & [Brendan](https://twitter.com/nerdenough) ( [Crystallize](https://crystallize.com/)), and [Simen](https://twitter.com/svale) ( [Sanity.io](https://www.sanity.io/)) shared insights on deployment best practices, frontend performance, and the value of improvisation in software development.


[Phil](https://twitter.com/GeneticGenesis) ( [Mux](https://mux.com/)), [Dan](https://twitter.com/DigitalizedDan) ( [Fauna](https://faunadb.com/)), and [Derrick](https://twitter.com/derrickreimer) (StaticKit) shared their experiences in cross-browser video, data persistence, and building businesses centered around frontend development.


[Belen Curcio](https://twitter.com/okbel) & [Dan Zajdband](https://twitter.com/impronunciable), [Kim](https://twitter.com/KimMaida) ( [Auth0](https://auth0.com/)), and [Zan](https://twitter.com/zmarkan) ( [Pusher](https://pusher.com/)) did the final set of talks, covering cutting-edge technologies like WebAssembly and the WebXR Device API, standards-based authentication, and uncovering how JAMstack and serverless are boosting productivity.


For the ending session, [Jared Palmer](https://twitter.com/jaredpalmer) and [Ken Wheeler](https://twitter.com/ken_wheeler) hosted a special episode of [The Undefined Podcast](https://undefined.fm/), discussing everything from isomorphic web apps to air purifiers — all in a casual setting!

## Sponsors

Without the support of our generous sponsors, putting backendlessConf\_ together wouldn't have been possible. Each of our sponsors play a unique, critical role within the JAMstack ecosystem:

- [**Cloudinary**](https://cloudinary.rocks/cejbz) — upload, manage, manipulate, and optimally serve any type of media

- [**Sanity**](https://www.sanity.io/) — take advantage of structured data to seamlessly deliver content across devices

- [**Mux**](https://mux.com/) — easily build & instantly stream video with straightforward APIs

- [**Prismic**](https://prismic.io/) — make your website editable by the whole team, effortlessly

- [**Pusher**](https://pusher.com/) — robust APIs for building scalable realtime communication features

- [**Crystallize**](https://crystallize.com/) — fast GraphQL-based APIs for tailor-made e-commerce

- [**FaunaDB**](https://fauna.com/) — low-friction serverless databases for all your Vercel projects
