# Preparing for AI’s economic impact: exploring policy responses

# 应对人工智能的经济影响：探索政策应对路径

Oct 14, 2025

2025年10月14日

![Preparing for AI’s economic impact: exploring policy responses](images/preparing-for-ai_s-economic-impact-exploring-policy-responses-anthropic/img_001.svg)

![应对人工智能的经济影响：探索政策应对路径](images/preparing-for-ai_s-economic-impact-exploring-policy-responses-anthropic/img_001.svg)

We tracked 11 observable behaviors across thousands of Claude.ai conversations to build the AI Fluency Index — a baseline for measuring how people collaborate with AI today.

我们追踪了数千场 Claude.ai 对话中的 11 种可观测行为，构建出“AI 熟练度指数”（AI Fluency Index）——该指数为衡量当今人类与 AI 协作水平提供了一个基准。

_How will the arrival of [powerful AI systems](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-s-recommendations-ostp-u-s-ai-action-plan) change the structure of the economy? We are uncertain, and so are external experts. But as AI systems continue to improve, and are adopted at an ever-larger scale, it’s crucial there is more discussion about the tools policymakers could use to respond to AI's economic impacts—whatever their nature. To help with this, we’re sharing several economic policy ideas that merit further study._

_强大的 AI 系统_[[1]](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-s-recommendations-ostp-u-s-ai-action-plan)_的到来将如何改变经济结构？我们尚不确定，外部专家也同样无法断言。但随着 AI 系统持续进步，并以日益扩大的规模被采纳应用，深入探讨政策制定者可用以应对 AI 经济影响（无论其具体形态如何）的工具，已变得至关重要。为此，我们提出若干值得进一步研究的经济政策思路。_

Since launching the [Anthropic Economic Index](https://www.anthropic.com/economic-index), we've observed an important shift in AI use. Users are becoming [increasingly likely to delegate full tasks to Claude](https://www.anthropic.com/research/anthropic-economic-index-september-2025-report), “collaborating” with Claude less. As AI models continue to work independently for longer periods of time, and as more employers adopt AI to improve their productivity, we expect this trend to accelerate. The implications for the workforce are uncertain.

自发布[Anthropic 经济指数](https://www.anthropic.com/economic-index)以来，我们观察到 AI 使用方式正发生一项重要转变：用户越来越倾向于将整项任务全权委托给 Claude 完成，而与 Claude 的“协作式”互动则相应减少。随着 AI 模型能够更长时间地独立运行，以及越来越多雇主采用 AI 提升自身生产力，我们预计这一趋势将进一步加速。其对劳动力市场的影响尚不明朗。

How should policymakers respond? This is not an easy question, nor is it one that any single actor can answer. There is great uncertainty about the scale of the transition ahead, and a wide range of views about how to manage it. But it is imperative to begin formulating ideas now for the economic scenarios we might find ourselves in.

政策制定者应如何回应？这并非一个容易回答的问题，也绝非任何单一行动方所能独自解答。我们对即将到来的转型规模尚存巨大不确定性，关于如何应对这一转型，各方观点也极为多元。但当务之急，是立即着手构想我们未来可能面临的各类经济情景。

Over the past year, we’ve worked with economists and policy experts from around the world (including members of our [Economic Advisory Council](https://www.anthropic.com/news/introducing-the-anthropic-economic-advisory-council) and participants in our [first Economic Futures Symposium](https://mccourt.georgetown.edu/news/anthropic-ai-symposium/)) to move this discussion forward. To generate a broad range of ideas, we’ve engaged with both non-partisan thinkers and those from across the political spectrum.

过去一年中，我们与来自全球的经济学家和政策专家（包括我们的[经济咨询委员会](https://www.anthropic.com/news/introducing-the-anthropic-economic-advisory-council)成员，以及首届[经济未来研讨会](https://mccourt.georgetown.edu/news/anthropic-ai-symposium/)的参会者）合作，推动这一讨论向前发展。为激发广泛多元的思想，我们既邀请了无党派学者，也积极听取了横跨政治光谱各立场人士的意见。

Below, we briefly explore nine of these categories of ideas, covering workforce development, permitting reform, fiscal policy, and social services.

下文简要探讨其中九类政策构想，涵盖劳动力发展、审批制度改革、财政政策及社会服务等领域。

While we don’t know what the optimal policies will prove to be, we’re committed to sharing ideas in the open, and to being transparent about the economic effects of advanced AI.

尽管我们尚无法确定何为最优政策，但我们承诺以开放姿态分享各类构想，并就先进人工智能所产生的经济影响保持透明。

## **Matching policies to scenarios**

## **依据不同情景匹配相应政策**

The rate, scale, and form of AI's economic effects will determine the policy responses that are necessary across the world. Accordingly, we've organized these initial ideas into three broad categories:

人工智能经济影响的发生速率、作用规模与具体形态，将决定全球各地所需采取的政策响应。因此，我们将这些初步构想划分为三大宽泛类别：

**Policy ideas for nearly all scenarios**, including those where negative effects on the labor market remain modest. These are policies that their advocates argue merit consideration almost regardless of how significant the disruption of AI proves to be. Given this, many of these proposals have been suggested in other contexts before. They include upskilling workers and students for emerging jobs, and reforming permitting processes to enable the construction of energy and computing infrastructure to improve productivity.

**适用于几乎所有情景的政策构想**，包括劳动力市场所受负面影响仍较轻微的情形。此类政策的支持者认为，无论人工智能引发的扰动最终严重程度如何，它们都值得认真考虑。正因如此，其中许多提议此前已在其他背景下被提出过。例如：面向新兴岗位提升劳动者与学生的技能水平；改革审批流程，以支持能源与算力基础设施建设，从而提升整体生产率。

**Policy ideas for scenarios with moderate acceleration**, where AI leads to measurable wage declines and job losses for large portions of the workforce. Here, more substantial fiscal support for displaced workers might be needed. To [offset negative externalities](https://www.nber.org/papers/w30154) imposed on displaced workers from rapid automation, taxes on automation might be considered in this scenario.

**适用于中等加速情景的政策构想**，即人工智能导致大范围劳动者出现可测量的薪资下降与失业。在此情形下，可能需要更有力的财政支持来援助受影响劳动者。为[抵消快速自动化给失业劳动者带来的负外部性](https://www.nber.org/papers/w30154)，本情景下亦可考虑开征自动化税。

**Policy ideas for faster-moving scenarios,** potentially involving dramatic job losses and worsening inequality. These proposals are much more ambitious, and are designed to respond to a starkly different economic picture. So far, ideas include using sovereign wealth funds to give citizens stakes in AI revenues, and finding new ways to generate government revenue.

**适用于快速演进情景的政策构想**，可能伴随大规模失业与日益加剧的不平等。此类提案更具雄心，旨在应对一种截然不同的经济图景。迄今提出的构想包括：利用主权财富基金，使公民分享人工智能产生的收益；探索政府收入的新来源。

The proposals below don’t necessarily represent Anthropic's own policy positions. But we’re excited by the breadth of proposals we’ve received, and we hope they encourage further research and debate.

以下所列提案未必代表Anthropic自身的政策立场。但我们欣喜于所收提案之广度，并期待它们能进一步激发研究与公共讨论。

## **Policies for nearly all scenarios**

## **适用于绝大多数场景的政策**

### **1\. Invest in upskilling through workforce training grants**

### **1. 通过劳动力培训补助金投资技能提升**

At our DC Symposium, Abigail Ball, Executive Director of [American Compass](https://americancompass.org/about/), presented the [Workforce Training Grant](https://americancompass.org/the-workforce-training-grant/)—a proposal she developed with colleague Oren Cass to direct public resources toward on-the-job training.

在我们的华盛顿特区（DC）研讨会上，[American Compass](https://americancompass.org/about/) 执行主任艾比盖尔·鲍尔（Abigail Ball）介绍了其与同事奥伦·卡斯（Oren Cass）共同提出的 [劳动力培训补助金](https://americancompass.org/the-workforce-training-grant/) 方案——该方案旨在将公共资金导向岗位实操型培训。

Under this model, governments would provide substantial annual subsidies (Ball and Cass suggest $10,000 per year in the US) directly to employers who create formal trainee positions with structured training programs. This training could take multiple forms: programs operated by individual employers, by employer consortia or industry associations, through partnerships between employers and organized labor, or by technical schools and community colleges working alongside employers.

根据该模式，政府将向设立正式学徒岗位并配套结构化培训计划的雇主，直接提供高额年度补贴（鲍尔与卡斯建议美国为每人每年1万美元）。此类培训可采取多种形式：由单个雇主自主开展；由雇主联盟或行业协会组织；由雇主与工会合作实施；或由职业技术学校及社区学院联合雇主共同推进。

American Compass proposes redirecting existing higher education subsidies to fund this program. But a range of other funding mechanisms might also deserve consideration—including the possibility of using taxes on AI consumption to support workforce development initiatives.

American Compass 建议将现有高等教育补贴转向资助该计划。但其他多种筹资机制也值得深入探讨——例如，考虑对人工智能消费征税，以支持劳动力发展相关举措。

### **2\. Reform tax incentives for worker retention and retraining**

### **2. 改革面向员工留任与再培训的税收激励政策**

Tax policy can, on the margin, incentivize employers to retrain and retain employees rather than reducing headcount.

税收政策可在边际层面激励雇主选择再培训和留用员工，而非裁员。

Revana Sharfuddin of the [Mercatus Center](https://www.mercatus.org/) argues that the US tax code creates a bias favoring physical capital investment over human capital investment. Businesses can immediately expense AI systems through bonus depreciation, yet face numerous restrictions when deducting worker training costs. She proposes reforms to the Internal Revenue Code, including eliminating the $5,250 cap on tax-free educational assistance, and extending full and immediate expensing to all job-related training.

[Mercatus 中心](https://www.mercatus.org/) 的雷瓦娜·沙尔富丁（Revana Sharfuddin）指出，美国税法存在偏向实物资本投资、忽视人力资本投资的制度性偏差：企业可通过“额外折旧”（bonus depreciation）立即全额列支人工智能系统购置成本，却在员工培训费用的税前扣除方面面临诸多限制。她提议修订《国内税收法典》（Internal Revenue Code），包括取消每年5,250美元的免税教育援助额度上限，并将全部与岗位相关的培训支出纳入全额、即时费用化（full and immediate expensing）范围。

These changes would aim to reduce the cost of retraining relative to the cost of layoffs, helping those workers whose positions might otherwise be on the margins of workforce reduction decisions.

上述调整旨在降低再培训成本相对于裁员成本的比重，从而助力那些岗位本可能处于裁员决策边缘的劳动者。

### **3\. Close corporate tax loopholes**

### **3. 堵住企业税收漏洞**

Tax policy expert [David Gamage](https://law.missouri.edu/directory/david-gamage/) has outlined reforms designed to prevent AI transformation from straining government budgets. Several of his proposals involve closing the “partnership gap” that allows large businesses to avoid entity-level taxes, and modernizing tax allocation to combat profit shifting and [better capture value](https://www.taxnotes.com/featured-analysis/reforming-state-corporate-income-taxes-can-yield-billions/2020/06/04/2cklk) from digital and intangibles-based business models.

税收政策专家[大卫·加马奇（David Gamage）](https://law.missouri.edu/directory/david-gamage/) 提出了一系列改革方案，旨在防止人工智能转型对政府财政预算造成压力。其中多项建议聚焦于弥合“合伙企业缺口”（partnership gap）——该漏洞使大型企业得以规避实体层面的税收；同时推动税制现代化，以遏制利润转移，并更有效地从以数字资产和无形资产为基础的商业模式中[捕获价值](https://www.taxnotes.com/featured-analysis/reforming-state-corporate-income-taxes-can-yield-billions/2020/06/04/2cklk)。

The second reform would allocate business taxes based on customer locations through market-based apportionment, while requiring worldwide combined reporting to treat multinationals and subsidiaries as single entities. This approach is designed to limit artificial profit-shifting to tax havens—a practice that could become more prevalent as AI potentially further increases the economic importance of profits derived from intangibles.

第二项改革将采用基于市场的应税所得分配方式（market-based apportionment），依据客户所在地来分配企业税负；同时要求跨国企业提交全球合并财务报告，将其海外子公司视作单一纳税实体。该方法旨在限制人为将利润转移至避税天堂的行为——而随着人工智能进一步提升无形资产所产生利润的经济重要性，此类行为可能愈发普遍。

Gamage argues that "governments that act first will solve their fiscal challenges and better position residents to thrive in an AI economy. Those that wait will face resource constraints when flexibility is most needed."

加马奇指出：“率先采取行动的政府将能有效应对财政挑战，并为其居民在人工智能驱动的经济中蓬勃发展奠定更坚实基础；而迟疑观望者，则将在最需要政策灵活性之时，面临资源严重受限的困境。”

### **4\. Accelerate permits and approvals for AI infrastructure**

### **4．加快人工智能基础设施的许可审批流程**

Anthropic has consistently advocated for [reforming permitting and power procurement processes in the United States](https://www.anthropic.com/news/build-ai-in-america) and allied nations. Accelerating these processes is needed to develop the infrastructure to train and deploy frontier AI—that is, large-scale data centers, transmission infrastructure, and power generation facilities. Reforms will also unlock investment, economic growth, and job creation in the places where AI is built. Failing to accelerate AI infrastructure development will slow productivity and job growth, and it could introduce national security risks from vital AI infrastructure moving offshore.

Anthropic（安西娅公司）一贯主张[改革美国](https://www.anthropic.com/news/build-ai-in-america)及盟国的项目许可与电力采购流程。加快这些流程，是建设用于训练和部署前沿人工智能（frontier AI）所需基础设施的必要之举——这包括大规模数据中心、输电基础设施以及发电设施。相关改革还将释放投资潜力，带动经济增长与就业创造，尤其惠及人工智能产业实际落地的地区。若未能加速人工智能基础设施建设，不仅将拖慢生产率与就业增长，还可能导致关键人工智能基础设施外迁，进而引发国家安全风险。

Three overlapping sets of U.S. regulatory processes delay building large-scale AI infrastructure for years. The first category is permits. These include a series of land use and environmental approvals at the federal, state, and local levels. Second, state regulatory reviews for transmission projects can cause buildouts of new lines to last 10 years or more. Finally, approvals to interconnect facilities to the electric grid typically take 4–6 years for generation resources.

美国存在三套相互交织的监管程序，致使大规模人工智能基础设施建设动辄延迟数年。第一类是各类许可，涵盖联邦、州及地方三级的土地使用审批与环境影响评估；第二类是各州针对输电项目的监管审查，常导致新建输电线路工程耗时长达十年以上；第三类是接入电网的并网许可，对于发电类资源，通常需耗时4至6年。

Concrete steps to address these challenges include reforms to the National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA), which requires federal agencies to review many projects’ environmental effects. Advance analyses of certain kinds of facilities, such as data centers, could help speed reviews of future projects. Other reforms could include leveraging federal authorities to expedite critical transmission buildouts and upgrades and collaboration with utilities to identify opportunities for fast interconnections.

应对上述挑战的具体举措包括：修订《国家环境政策法》（National Environmental Policy Act, NEPA）——该法案要求联邦机构对众多项目的环境影响进行评估；可对特定类型设施（如数据中心）开展预先环境影响分析，从而加快后续同类项目的审批进度。其他改革还可包括：授权联邦机构加快关键输电线路的新建与升级进程，并协同公用事业公司识别快速并网的可行机会。

As [Tyler Cowen](https://www.mercatus.org/scholars/tyler-cowen), faculty director of the Mercatus Center and member of our Economic Advisory Council, notes: "I am all for permitting reform—the energy sector included."

正如梅卡图斯中心（Mercatus Center）教员主任、同时也是我们经济顾问委员会成员的[泰勒·考恩（Tyler Cowen）](https://www.mercatus.org/scholars/tyler-cowen)所言：“我全力支持许可制度改革——能源领域亦不例外。”

## **Policy ideas for moderate scenarios**

## **适用于中度情景的政策建议**

### **5\. Establish trade adjustment assistance for AI displacement**

### **5．设立面向人工智能所致失业的贸易调整援助机制**

Several economists are exploring how the [Trade Adjustment Assistance (TAA) model](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/eta/tradeact/about) – in which affected workers are given opportunities to obtain new skills, or receive other support – might be adapted for labor market disruptions in an era of powerful AI. [Ioana Marinescu](https://sp2.upenn.edu/person/ioana-e-marinescu/) of the University of Pennsylvania, a member of our Economic Advisory Council, views TAA-like "AI insurance" as a mechanism "to support those who lose jobs due to AI."

多位经济学家正在探索如何将[贸易调整援助（TAA）模式](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/eta/tradeact/about)——即为受冲击工人提供获取新技能的机会或其他支持——适配于强人工智能时代所引发的劳动力市场扰动。宾夕法尼亚大学的[伊奥安娜·马林斯库（Ioana Marinescu）](https://sp2.upenn.edu/person/ioana-e-marinescu/)教授（我们经济顾问委员会成员）将类似TAA的“AI保险”视为一种机制，用以“支持因AI而失业的劳动者”。

Along these lines, [Suchet Mittal](https://www.linkedin.com/in/suchet-mittal?miniProfileUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_miniProfile%3AACoAACWdppsBYap_1fiHL6GFTidKtDTSxhVGEvI&lipi=urn%3Ali%3Apage%3Ad_flagship3_search_srp_people%3BhHHJ%2FfZ5SvGjBHHBE%2Fxf%2Bw%3D%3D) and [Sam Manning](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=-gjxfJgAAAAJ&hl=en) have outlined a potential Automation Adjustment Assistance (AAA) program. They describe how funding AAA at levels similar to TAA—approximately $700 million annually—could be an initial option, with mechanisms built in to increase or decrease the size of the program in line with the pace and scale of AI-driven displacement.

在此基础上，[苏切特·米塔尔（Suchet Mittal）](https://www.linkedin.com/in/suchet-mittal?miniProfileUrn=urn%3Ali%3Afs_miniProfile%3AACoAACWdppsBYap_1fiHL6GFTidKtDTSxhVGEvI&lipi=urn%3Ali%3Apage%3Ad_flagship3_search_srp_people%3BhHHJ%2FfZ5SvGjBHHBE%2Fxf%2Bw%3D%3D)与[萨姆·曼宁（Sam Manning）](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=-gjxfJgAAAAJ&hl=en)提出了一个潜在的“自动化调整援助（AAA）计划”。他们指出，初期可参照TAA的资助规模（约每年7亿美元）为AAA提供资金，并内建动态调节机制，使该计划的规模能随AI驱动型岗位替代的速度与广度同步增减。

Mittal and Manning note that if such a program needed to expand in the future, it could potentially be funded through taxes on AI-driven revenues from firms above a certain high level of market capitalization, creating a direct mechanism for the AI sector to support workers displaced by the technology.

米塔尔与曼宁指出，若未来该计划需扩大规模，其资金来源或可考虑对市值达到某一较高门槛的企业所获得的AI驱动型收入征税，从而建立AI产业直接反哺被该技术取代工人的机制。

### **6\. Implement taxes on compute or token generation**

### **6. 对算力或Token生成征税**

University of Virginia economists Lee Lockwood and Anton Korinek (a member of our [Economic Advisory Council](https://www.anthropic.com/news/introducing-the-anthropic-economic-advisory-council)) propose [studying of a range of taxes](https://www.nber.org/research/videos/2025-economics-transformative-ai-workshop-public-finance-age-ai-primer) on “token generation, robots, robot services, and digital services.”

弗吉尼亚大学经济学家李·洛克伍德（Lee Lockwood）与安东·科里内克（Anton Korinek）（我们[经济顾问委员会](https://www.anthropic.com/news/introducing-the-anthropic-economic-advisory-council)成员）提议[研究一系列税制方案](https://www.nber.org/research/videos/2025-economics-transformative-ai-workshop-public-finance-age-ai-primer)，涵盖“Token生成、机器人、机器人服务及数字服务”。

These taxes offer different potential benefits – and distortionary risks – depending on the stage of AI’s development within the economy. A tax on AI-generated tokens sold to end users (a “token tax”) might be desirable when humans remain dominant consumers in the economy, even if powerful AI reduces the relative economic role of labor.

此类税收在不同AI经济发展阶段下，具有各异的潜在收益与扭曲风险。当人类仍是经济中主导性消费者时（即便强人工智能已相对削弱劳动力的经济地位），对销售给终端用户的AI生成Token征税（即“Token税”）可能较为适宜。

Korinek and Lockwood argue that, if the economy reaches a stage where powerful AI systems become themselves major consumers of the economy’s resources, taxing AI resource accumulation–e.g. via taxes on compute and other hardware–might be more effective than token taxes on human end users. Although these taxes on computational resources distort investment along an AI-transformed economy’s trajectory, they could become the only remaining mechanism to capture some of the windfall generated by AI if the role of both labor markets and human consumption in the economy declines.

科里内克与洛克伍德认为，倘若经济演进至强人工智能系统自身成为经济资源主要消耗者的新阶段，则对AI资源积累（例如通过针对算力及其他硬件征税）征税，或将比对人类终端用户征收Token税更为有效。尽管此类算力相关税收会在AI转型经济的发展路径上扭曲投资决策，但一旦劳动力市场与人类消费在经济中的作用双双衰退，它可能将成为捕获AI所创造部分超额收益的唯一可行机制。

We believe taxes in this broader category deserve serious study, even though they would directly impact Anthropic's revenue and profitability. These taxes could provide crucial revenue for vital fiscal programs—including several others discussed in this post.

我们认为，这一更广泛类别的税收值得深入研究，即便其将直接影响Anthropic的营收与盈利能力。此类税收可为关键财政项目提供至关重要的资金支持——包括本文所讨论的其他多项政策建议。

## **Policy ideas for fast-moving scenarios**

## **面向快速演进情境的政策构想**

### **7\. Create national sovereign wealth funds with stakes in AI**

### **7. 设立国家主权财富基金，持有AI领域股权**

A growing set of proposals aims to give citizens and governments greater stakes in AI's economic returns. Sovereign wealth funds could enable states to acquire positions in AI-related assets. In scenarios where the AI sector captures an outsized share of economic wealth, government investment could both shape the sector's behavior and " [distribute AI-derived wealth more equitably](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5343934)."

越来越多的政策提案旨在让公民和政府在人工智能所产生的经济回报中拥有更大权益。主权财富基金可助力各国获取与人工智能相关的资产权益。在人工智能产业攫取不成比例的经济财富的情形下，政府投资既可引导该产业的行为方向，亦可“[更公平地分配人工智能所创造的财富](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5343934)”。

Writing for the Centre for British Progress, Emma Casey, Emma Rockall, and Helena Roy have proposed a related concept for the United Kingdom: [an AI Bond](https://britishprogress.org/uk-day-one/designing-an-ai-bond-for-growth-and-shared-prosper). The AI Bond would aim to ensure adequate investment in "the AI stack" to capture AI's benefits and then distribute its returns more evenly across Britain—even as AI research roles concentrate in a few cities, like London.

英国进步中心（Centre for British Progress）撰稿人艾玛·凯西（Emma Casey）、艾玛·罗卡爾（Emma Rockall）与海伦娜·罗伊（Helena Roy）为英国提出了一项相关构想：[人工智能债券（AI Bond）](https://britishprogress.org/uk-day-one/designing-an-ai-bond-for-growth-and-shared-prosper)。该债券旨在确保对“人工智能技术栈（the AI stack）”进行充分投资，以充分捕获人工智能带来的收益，并将这些收益更均衡地分配至全英各地——即便人工智能研发岗位正日益向伦敦等少数城市集聚。

### **8\. Adopt or modernize value-added taxes**

### **8．采纳或更新增值税制度**

Six out of the G7 countries have national value-added taxes (VATs), as do 37 out of 38 OECD countries. The United States is the exception.

七国集团（G7）中六个国家已设立全国性增值税（VAT），经合组织（OECD）38个成员国中亦有37个实行增值税。美国是唯一例外。

As AI transforms the economy, labor's share of the production of value might decline significantly. A shift toward taxing consumption (as through a VAT) could become necessary to fund core government activities. VAT collection also provides governments with fine-grained information about the economic production network—which could be particularly valuable during this potential period of rapid technological and economic change.

随着人工智能重塑经济结构，劳动要素在价值创造中所占份额可能大幅下降。转向以消费为税基（例如通过增值税）的征税模式，或将变得必要，以保障政府核心职能的财政支撑。此外，增值税征管过程还能为政府提供关于经济生产网络的精细化数据——在当前这一技术与经济可能加速变革的关键时期，此类信息尤为宝贵。

"Value-added taxes are non-distortionary and to an extent, self-enforcing," notes John Horton of MIT's Sloan School of Management, a member of our Economic Advisory Council.

麻省理工学院斯隆管理学院（MIT Sloan School of Management）教授、本项目经济咨询委员会成员约翰·霍顿（John Horton）指出：“增值税具有非扭曲性，且在一定程度上具备自我执行特征。”

### **9\. Implement new revenue structures to account for AI’s growing share of the economy**

### **9．构建新型税收机制，以应对人工智能在国民经济中占比持续上升的趋势**

If AI is responsible for a large share of economic output (causing labor’s share to decline), governments might require new revenue streams to complement income tax. Another of David Gamage’s proposals is exploring a "low-rate business wealth tax" as a complement to income taxes. His reasoning: "Income taxes face accounting manipulation; wealth taxes face asset valuation challenges. Using both makes the system harder to avoid" for highly profitable enterprises.

倘若人工智能贡献了相当大比例的经济产出（从而导致劳动收入占比下降），各国政府可能需要开辟新的财政收入来源，以补充个人所得税体系。戴维·加马奇（David Gamage）提出的另一项建议，是探索开征一种“低税率企业财富税”，作为对所得税的补充。其理由在于：“所得税易受会计操纵影响，而财富税则面临资产估值难题；二者并用，可显著提升高盈利企业的避税难度。”

Gamage analogizes this system to the fee structures that certain asset managers charge clients: "the wealth tax functions as a management fee for providing legal infrastructure protecting accumulated capital, while the income tax serves as a performance fee for profits generated in state markets." This idea represents one way that governments might adapt to changes in the value of human labor, although we think there are many more ideas to be explored in this area.

加马奇将这一制度类比于某些资产管理公司向客户收取的费用结构：“财富税相当于为维护保障资本积累的法律基础设施所支付的管理费，而所得税则相当于就企业在国家市场中所获利润所支付的业绩报酬。”这一构想代表了政府应对人类劳动价值变化的一种可能路径；不过我们认为，该领域尚有大量创新思路有待进一步探索。

None of the ideas outlined here represent definitive recommendations. They are starting points for deeper research, policy development, and public debate. The economic effects of AI remain uncertain in both timing and magnitude, and different scenarios will require different responses.

本文所列各项构想均不构成最终定论性建议。它们仅是推动更深入研究、政策制定与公共讨论的起点。人工智能的经济影响在发生时点与作用强度两方面仍存在不确定性，不同情景亦需采取差异化的应对策略。

What's clear, though, is that proactive engagement between researchers, policymakers, and the AI industry is essential. By exploring these options now—before we know the shape of AI’s economic effects—we can better prepare for a range of possible futures, and ensure that workers and communities are well-placed to benefit from the full potential of AI.

但有一点十分明确：研究人员、政策制定者与人工智能产业之间必须开展主动协作。唯有在人工智能经济影响的具体形态尚不明朗之际，就着手探讨这些选项，我们才能为多种可能的未来做好更充分准备，并确保劳动者与社区能够切实受益于人工智能的全部潜力。
