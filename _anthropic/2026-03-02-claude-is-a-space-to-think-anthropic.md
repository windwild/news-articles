# Claude is a space to think

# Claude 是一个思考的空间

Feb 4, 2026

2026 年 2 月 4 日

![Claude is a space to think](images/claude-is-a-space-to-think-anthropic/img_001.svg)

![Claude 是一个思考的空间](images/claude-is-a-space-to-think-anthropic/img_001.svg)












Advertising drives competition, helps people discover new products, and allows services like email and social media to be offered for free. We’ve run our own [ad campaigns](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FDNkDBNR7AM), and our AI models have, in turn, helped many of our customers in the advertising industry.

广告推动市场竞争，帮助用户发现新产品，并使电子邮件、社交媒体等服务得以免费提供。我们自身也开展过[广告活动](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FDNkDBNR7AM)，而我们的 AI 模型反过来也助力了众多广告行业的客户。

But including ads in conversations with Claude would be incompatible with what we want Claude to be: a genuinely helpful assistant for work and for deep thinking.

但在与 Claude 的对话中插入广告，将与我们希望 Claude 成为的形象相冲突：一个真正有助于工作和深度思考的助手。

We want Claude to act unambiguously in our users’ interests. So we’ve made a choice: Claude will remain ad-free. Our users won’t see “sponsored” links adjacent to their conversations with Claude; nor will Claude’s responses be influenced by advertisers or include third-party product placements our users did not ask for.

我们希望 Claude 始终明确地以用户利益为行动准则。因此，我们做出了一个决定：Claude 将始终保持无广告状态。我们的用户不会在与 Claude 的对话旁看到“赞助”链接；Claude 的回复也不会受广告商影响，更不会包含用户并未主动要求的第三方产品植入。

## **The nature of AI conversations**

## **AI 对话的本质**

When people use search engines or social media, they’ve come to expect a mixture of organic and sponsored content. Filtering signal from noise is part of the interaction.

当人们使用搜索引擎或社交媒体时，已习惯于同时面对自然内容与推广内容的混合呈现。“去噪取信”本身即构成交互体验的一部分。

Conversations with AI assistants are meaningfully different. The format is open-ended; users often share context and reveal more than they would in a search query. This openness is part of what makes conversations with AI valuable, but it’s also what makes them susceptible to influence in ways that other digital products are not.

而与 AI 助手的对话则存在本质差异：其形式是开放式的；用户常常主动提供背景信息，透露的内容远超一次普通搜索所能承载。这种开放性正是 AI 对话价值所在，却也正因如此，它比其他数字产品更容易受到隐性影响。

Our [analysis of conversations](https://www.anthropic.com/news/how-people-use-claude-for-support-advice-and-companionship) with Claude (conducted in a way that keeps all data [private and anonymous](https://www.anthropic.com/research/clio)) shows that an appreciable portion involve topics that are sensitive or deeply personal—the kinds of conversations you might have with a trusted advisor. Many other uses involve complex software engineering tasks, deep work, or thinking through difficult problems. The appearance of ads in these contexts would feel incongruous—and, in many cases, inappropriate.

我们对用户与 Claude 的对话所开展的[分析研究](https://www.anthropic.com/news/how-people-use-claude-for-support-advice-and-companionship)（全程确保所有数据均[私密且匿名](https://www.anthropic.com/research/clio)）显示，相当一部分对话涉及敏感或高度私密的话题——这类话题通常只会在你与一位值得信赖的顾问之间展开。其余大量使用场景则涵盖复杂的软件工程任务、深度知识工作，或对棘手问题的系统性思考。在这些语境中出现广告，不仅显得格格不入，而且在许多情况下实属不当。

We still have much to learn about the impact of AI models on the people who use them. [Early](https://www.jmir.org/2025/1/e67114) [research](https://hai.stanford.edu/news/exploring-the-dangers-of-ai-in-mental-health-care) suggests both benefits—like people finding support they couldn’t access elsewhere—and risks, including the potential for models to reinforce harmful beliefs in vulnerable users. Introducing advertising incentives at this stage would add another level of complexity. [Our understanding](https://www.anthropic.com/research/tracing-thoughts-language-model) of how models translate the goals we set them into specific behaviors is still developing; an ad-based system could therefore have unpredictable results.

关于 AI 模型对使用者的实际影响，我们仍有许多未知有待探索。[早期](https://www.jmir.org/2025/1/e67114) [研究](https://hai.stanford.edu/news/exploring-the-dangers-of-ai-in-mental-health-care)已初步揭示其双重性：一方面带来切实益处——例如帮助人们获得原本难以获取的心理支持；另一方面亦潜藏风险——包括可能在易感人群中强化有害信念。在此阶段引入广告驱动的激励机制，将使问题进一步复杂化。我们对模型如何将人类设定的目标转化为具体行为的理解仍在演进之中（参见[相关研究](https://www.anthropic.com/research/tracing-thoughts-language-model)）；因此，一套以广告为核心的系统，其实际效果可能难以预测。

## **Incentive structures**

## **激励机制**

Being genuinely helpful is one of the core principles of [Claude’s Constitution](https://www.anthropic.com/constitution), the document that describes our vision for Claude’s character and guides how we train the model. An advertising-based business model would introduce incentives that could work against this principle.

“真正提供帮助”是[《Claude 宪章》](https://www.anthropic.com/constitution)的核心原则之一——这份文件定义了我们对 Claude 人格特质的构想，并指导着模型的训练方向。而基于广告的商业模式，将引入可能与此原则相悖的激励机制。

Consider a concrete example. A user mentions they’re having trouble sleeping. An assistant without advertising incentives would explore the various potential causes—stress, environment, habits, and so on—based on what might be most insightful to the user. An ad-supported assistant has an additional consideration: whether the conversation presents an opportunity to make a transaction. These objectives may often align—but not always. And, unlike a list of search results, ads that influence a model’s responses may make it difficult to tell whether a given recommendation comes with a commercial motive or not. Users shouldn’t have to second-guess whether an AI is genuinely helping them or subtly steering the conversation towards something monetizable.

试想一个具体例子：用户提到自己最近失眠。一个不受广告激励影响的助手，会围绕压力、环境、生活习惯等潜在成因展开探讨，一切以对用户最具启发性为依归；而一个依赖广告收入的助手，则需额外权衡：当前对话是否提供了促成交易的机会。这两类目标虽常一致，却未必总能兼容。更重要的是，不同于搜索引擎中清晰分隔的“自然结果”与“广告位”，若广告逻辑渗透至模型的生成过程，用户将难以分辨某条建议究竟源于真诚协助，还是暗含商业动因。用户本不应被迫反复揣测：眼前的 AI 究竟是真心助人，还是在不动声色地将对话引向可变现的方向。

Even ads that don’t directly influence an AI model’s responses and instead appear separately within the chat window would compromise what we want Claude to be: a clear space to think and work. Such ads would also introduce an incentive to optimize for engagement—for the amount of time people spend using Claude and how often they return. These metrics aren’t necessarily aligned with being genuinely helpful. The most useful AI interaction might be a short one, or one that resolves the user’s request without prompting further conversation.

即使那些并不直接影响 AI 模型响应、而仅在聊天窗口中独立展示的广告，也会损害我们希望 Claude 成为的样子：一个清晰、专注的思考与工作空间。此类广告还会催生一种以“用户参与度”为导向的激励机制——即追求用户使用 Claude 的时长及其回访频率。然而，这些指标未必与真正提供帮助的目标相一致。最有价值的 AI 交互，可能恰恰是一次简短的对话，或一次无需后续追问便直接解决用户需求的交互。

We recognize that not all advertising implementations are equivalent. More transparent or opt-in approaches—where users explicitly choose to see sponsored content—might avoid some of the concerns outlined above. But the history of ad-supported products suggests that advertising incentives, once introduced, tend to expand over time as they become integrated into revenue targets and product development, blurring boundaries that were once more clear-cut. We’ve chosen not to introduce these dynamics into Claude.

我们深知，并非所有广告实现方式都等同。更具透明度或采用“用户主动选择”（opt-in）的方式——即用户明确同意查看赞助内容——或许能在一定程度上规避上述部分担忧。但广告支持型产品的历史表明：一旦引入广告激励机制，它往往会随时间推移不断扩张，逐步融入企业的营收目标与产品开发流程之中，从而模糊原本清晰的边界。正因如此，我们决定不在 Claude 中引入这类机制。

## **Our approach**

## **我们的路径**

Anthropic is focused on businesses, developers, and helping our users flourish. Our business model is straightforward: we generate revenue through enterprise contracts and paid subscriptions, and we reinvest that revenue into improving Claude for our users. This is a choice with tradeoffs, and we respect that other AI companies might reasonably reach different conclusions.

Anthropic 的核心服务对象是企业客户、开发者，以及助力广大用户蓬勃发展。我们的商业模式简洁明了：通过企业级合同与付费订阅获取收入，并将所得收益持续投入于 Claude 的优化升级，以更好地服务用户。这一选择意味着权衡取舍；我们亦充分尊重其他 AI 公司基于自身判断所作出的不同决策。

Expanding access to Claude is central to our public benefit mission, and we want to do it without selling our users’ attention or data to advertisers. To that end, we’ve [brought AI tools and training to educators](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-teach-for-all) in over 60 countries, begun national AI education pilots with [multiple](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-and-iceland-announce-one-of-the-world-s-first-national-ai-education-pilots) [governments](https://www.anthropic.com/news/rwandan-government-partnership-ai-education), and made Claude [available to nonprofits](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-nonprofits) at a significant discount. We continue to invest in our smaller models so that our free offering remains at the frontier of intelligence, and we may consider lower-cost subscription tiers and regional pricing where there is clear demand for it. Should we need to revisit this approach, we’ll be transparent about our reasons for doing so.

扩大 Claude 的可及性，是我们践行公共福祉使命的核心所在；而我们希望在不向广告商出售用户注意力或数据的前提下实现这一目标。为此，我们已将 AI 工具与培训资源带入全球 **60 多个国家** 的教育工作者群体；并与**多个政府**（包括[冰岛](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-and-iceland-announce-one-of-the-world-s-first-national-ai-education-pilots)、[卢旺达](https://www.anthropic.com/news/rwandan-government-partnership-ai-education)等）共同启动国家级 AI 教育试点项目；同时面向非营利组织大幅优惠提供 [Claude 服务](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-nonprofits)。我们持续投资于更轻量级模型的研发，确保免费版 Claude 始终处于智能能力的前沿水平；未来，若市场需求明确，我们也可能推出更具性价比的订阅层级及区域性定价方案。倘若未来需要重新审视这一路径，我们将坦诚说明调整原因。

## **Supporting commerce**

## **赋能商业活动**

AI will increasingly interact with commerce, and we look forward to supporting this in ways that help our users. We’re particularly interested in the potential of agentic commerce, where Claude acts on a user’s behalf to handle a purchase or booking end to end. And we’ll continue to build features that enable our users to find, compare, or buy products, connect with businesses, and more—when they choose to do so.

AI 将日益深入地融入商业活动，我们期待以切实助力用户的方式支持这一趋势。我们尤其关注“自主代理式商业”（agentic commerce）的潜力——即由 Claude 代表用户，端到端完成购物或预订等操作。此外，我们也将持续构建各类功能，使用户在**主动选择时**，能够便捷地查找、比价、购买商品，对接商家，乃至开展更多商业相关活动。

We’re also exploring more ways to make Claude a focused space to be at your most productive. Users can already [connect third-party tools](https://claude.com/blog/interactive-tools-in-claude) they use for work—like Figma, Asana, and Canva—and interact with them directly within Claude. We expect to introduce many more useful integrations and expand this toolkit over time.

我们还在积极探索更多方式，让 Claude 成为助您高效专注的工作空间。目前，用户已可[接入日常工作中使用的第三方工具](https://claude.com/blog/interactive-tools-in-claude)，例如 Figma、Asana 和 Canva，并直接在 Claude 内与其交互。未来，我们将陆续推出大量实用集成，并持续拓展这一工具集。

All third-party interactions will be grounded in the same overarching design principle: they should be initiated by the _user_(where the AI is working for them) rather than an _advertiser_ (where the AI is working, at least in part, for someone else). Today, whether someone asks Claude to research running shoes, compare mortgage rates, or recommend a restaurant for a special occasion, Claude’s only incentive is to give a helpful answer. We’d like to preserve that.

所有第三方交互均遵循同一根本设计原则：必须由**用户主动发起**（此时 AI 是为用户服务），而非由**广告商驱动**（此时 AI 至少部分地为他人服务）。当前，无论用户请 Claude 研究跑鞋、比较房贷利率，还是为特别场合推荐餐厅，Claude 唯一的动机始终是提供真正有帮助的回答。我们希望长久守护这一初心。

## **A trusted tool for thought**

## **值得信赖的思维伙伴**

We want our users to trust Claude to help them keep thinking—about their work, their challenges, and their ideas.

我们希望用户能够信任 Claude，让它持续助力他们思考——思考自己的工作、面临的挑战以及各种创意想法。

Our experience of using the internet has made it easy to assume that advertising on the products we use is inevitable. But open a notebook, pick up a well-crafted tool, or stand in front of a clean chalkboard, and there are no ads in sight.

我们使用互联网的长期体验，使人们很容易认为：自己所使用的各类产品中必然充斥着广告。但当你打开一本笔记本、拿起一件精心设计的工具，或站在一块洁净的黑板前时，却完全看不到任何广告。

We think Claude should work the same way.

我们认为，Claude 也理应如此运作。
