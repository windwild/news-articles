## **Geography**

## **地理分布**

We've expanded the Anthropic Economic Index to include geographic data. Below we cover what we've learned about how Claude is used across countries and US states.

我们已将地理维度数据纳入《Anthropic 经济指数》，以全面刻画 Claude 在不同国家及美国各州的使用图景。以下内容汇总了我们在跨国及跨州层面的重要发现。

### Across countries

### 全球各国间

The US uses Claude far more than any other nation. India is in second place, followed by Brazil, Japan, and South Korea, each with similar shares.

美国使用 Claude 的程度远超其他任何国家。印度位居第二，其后依次为巴西、日本和韩国，四国所占份额相近。

Leading countries in terms of global Claude.ai use share.

全球范围内 Claude.ai 使用量占比最高的国家。

However, there is huge variation in population size across these countries. To account for this, we adjust each country’s share of Claude.ai use by its share of the world’s working population. This gives us our **Anthropic AI Usage Index**, or AUI. Countries with an AUI greater than 1 use Claude more often than we’d expect based on their working-age population alone, and vice-versa.

然而，上述各国人口规模差异巨大。为消除这一影响，我们以各国劳动年龄人口占全球劳动年龄人口的比例为基准，对各国 Claude.ai 使用量占比进行标准化调整，由此得出我们的 **Anthropic AI 使用指数**（Anthropic AI Usage Index，简称 AUI）。AUI 值大于 1 的国家，其 Claude 使用频率高于仅按劳动年龄人口规模所预期的水平；反之亦然。

![The twenty countries that score highest on our Anthropic AI Usage Index: Israel, Singapore, Australia, New Zealand, and South Korea are the top five.](https://www.anthropic.com/_next/image?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww-cdn.anthropic.com%2Fimages%2F4zrzovbb%2Fwebsite%2F5303d8780f4f566e994b12b2d0549947160711ba-6308x4197.png&w=3840&q=75)Anthropic AI 使用指数排名前二十的国家：以色列、新加坡、澳大利亚、新西兰与韩国位列前五。

The twenty countries that score highest on our Anthropic AI Usage Index.

Anthropic AI 使用指数得分最高的二十个国家。

From the AUI data, we can see that some small, technologically advanced countries (like Israel and Singapore) lead in Claude adoption relative to their working-age populations. This might to a large degree be explained by income: we found a strong correlation between GDP per capita and the Anthropic AI Usage Index (a 1% higher GDP per capita was associated with a 0.7% higher AUI). This makes sense: the countries that use Claude most often generally also have robust internet connectivity, as well as economies oriented around knowledge work rather than manufacturing. But it does raise a question of economic divergence: previous general-purpose technologies, like electrification or the combustion engine, led to both vast economic growth and a [great divergence](https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.11.3.3) in living standards around the world. If the effects of AI prove to be largest in richer countries, this general-purpose technology might have similar economic implications.

从 AUI 数据可见，一些国土面积较小但科技高度发达的国家（如以色列和新加坡）在人均劳动年龄人口基础上的 Claude 采用率位居前列。这一现象在很大程度上可能由收入水平解释：我们发现人均 GDP 与 Anthropic AI 使用指数之间存在显著正相关关系（人均 GDP 每提高 1%，AUI 平均上升 0.7%）。这不难理解——Claude 使用最频繁的国家，通常也具备强劲的互联网连接能力，且经济结构以知识型工作为主，而非制造业。但这也引出一个关于经济分化的关键问题：以往的通用技术（如电气化或内燃机）既推动了巨大的经济增长，也导致了全球生活水平的[“大分化”](https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.11.3.3)。倘若人工智能的影响在富裕国家最为显著，那么这项通用技术也可能带来类似的经济后果。

### Patterns within the United States

### 美国各州内部的使用模式

The link between per capita GDP and per capita use of Claude also holds when comparing between US states. In fact, use rises more quickly within income here than across countries: a 1% higher per capita GDP inside the US is associated with a 1.8% higher population-adjusted use of Claude. That said, income actually has _less_ explanatory power within the US than across countries, as there’s much higher variance within the overall trend. That is: other factors, beyond income, must explain more of the variation in population-adjusted use.

人均 GDP 与人均 Claude 使用量之间的关联，在美国各州之间同样成立。事实上，在州际层面，这一关联强度甚至高于国家层面：美国境内人均 GDP 每提升 1%，经人口规模调整后的 Claude 使用量便相应上升 1.8%。但需注意的是，相较于跨国比较，收入在美国各州层面的解释力反而更弱——因为整体趋势中存在更大的离散性。换言之：除收入之外的其他因素，对人口调整后使用量的差异具有更强的解释力。

What else could explain this adoption gap? Our best guess is that it’s differences in the composition of states’ economies. The highest AUI in the US is the District of Columbia (3.82), where the most disproportionately frequent uses of Claude are editing documents and searching for information, among other tasks associated with knowledge work in DC. Similarly, coding-related tasks are especially common in California (the state with the third-highest AUI overall), and finance-related tasks are especially common in New York (which comes in fourth).1 Even among states with lower population-adjusted use of Claude, like Hawaii, use is closely correlated to the structure of the economy: people in Hawaii request Claude’s assistance for tourism-related tasks at twice the rate of the rest of America. Our [interactive website](http://anthropic.com/economic-index) contains plenty of other statistics like these.

还有哪些因素可能解释这一采用率差距？我们最合理的推测是各州经济结构的差异。美国 AUI 最高的地区是华盛顿哥伦比亚特区（DC），达 3.82；当地用户使用 Claude 最突出的场景包括文档编辑与信息检索等，均属特区典型的知识型工作范畴。类似地，编程相关任务在加利福尼亚州（全美 AUI 排名第三）尤为普遍；而纽约州（AUI 排名第四）则以金融相关任务使用率最高见长。¹ 即便在人均调整后 Claude 使用率较低的州（如夏威夷），其使用模式仍与本地经济结构高度吻合：夏威夷居民请求 Claude 协助旅游相关任务的频率，是全美平均水平的两倍。我们的[交互式网站](http://anthropic.com/economic-index)还提供了大量此类统计数据。

## **Trends in Claude use**

## **Claude 使用趋势**

We’ve been tracking how people use Claude since December 2024. We use a privacy-preserving [classification method](https://www.anthropic.com/research/clio) that categorizes anonymized conversation transcripts into task groups defined by O\*NET, a US government database that classifies jobs and the tasks associated with them.² By doing this, we can analyze both how the tasks that people give Claude have changed since last year, and how the ways people choose to collaborate—how much oversight and input into Claude’s work they choose to have—have changed too.

我们自 2024 年 12 月以来持续追踪用户使用 Claude 的方式。我们采用一种注重隐私保护的[分类方法](https://www.anthropic.com/research/clio)，将匿名化处理后的对话记录，依据美国劳工部运营的职业与任务分类数据库 O\*NET 所定义的任务类别进行归类。² 借此，我们既能分析用户交付给 Claude 的任务类型相较于去年发生了哪些变化，也能考察人们协作方式的演变——即用户对 Claude 工作过程所施加的监督程度与主动介入程度如何发生变化。

### Tasks

### 任务类型

Since December 2024, computer and mathematical uses of Claude have predominated among our categories, representing around 37–40% of conversations.

自 2024 年 12 月以来，在所有任务类别中，“计算机与数学类”用途始终占据主导地位，约占全部对话量的 37%–40%。

But a lot has changed. Over the past nine months, we’ve seen consistent growth in “knowledge-intensive” fields. For example, educational instruction tasks have risen by more than 40 percent (from 9% to 13% of all conversations), and the share of tasks associated with the physical and social sciences has increased by a third (from 6% to 8%). In the meantime, the relative frequency of traditional business tasks has declined: management-related tasks have fallen from 5% of all conversations to 3%, and the share of tasks related to business and financial operations has halved, from 6% to 3%. (In absolute terms, of course, the number of conversations in each category has still risen significantly.)

但诸多变化已然发生。过去九个月中，“知识密集型”领域呈现持续增长态势。例如，教育指导类任务增幅超过 40%（占全部对话的比例从 9% 上升至 13%），而物理与社会科学相关任务的占比则增长了三分之一（从 6% 提升至 8%）。与此同时，传统商业类任务的相对占比则有所下降：管理类任务占比由全部对话的 5% 下降至 3%，商业与金融运营类任务占比更是减半，从 6% 降至 3%。（当然，就绝对数量而言，各类别下的对话总数仍显著增长。）

尽管如此，软件开发仍是我们在所有被追踪国家中观察到的最常见用途。在美国亦是如此，但由于样本规模所限，我们尚难以深入探究任务构成随 AI 采纳率变化而产生的具体差异。

### Patterns of interaction

### 交互模式

As we’ve discussed [previously](https://www.anthropic.com/news/the-anthropic-economic-index), we generally distinguish between tasks that involve _automation_ (in which AI directly produces work with minimal user input) and _augmentation_ (in which the user and AI collaborate to get things done).  
正如我们此前[讨论过](https://www.anthropic.com/news/the-anthropic-economic-index)的那样，我们通常将任务划分为两类：**自动化**（AI 在极少用户输入的情况下直接产出工作成果）和**增强辅助**（用户与 AI 协作完成任务）。

We further break automation down into _directive_ and _feedback loop_ interactions, where directive conversations involve the minimum of human interaction, and in feedback loop tasks, humans relay real-world outcomes back to the model.  
我们进一步将自动化细分为**指令式交互**（directive interactions）和**反馈闭环式交互**（feedback loop interactions）：前者要求人类干预最少；后者则需人类将现实世界的结果反馈给模型。

We also break augmentation down into _learning_ (asking for information or explanations), _task iteration_ (working with Claude collaboratively), and _validation_ (asking for feedback).  
同样，我们将增强辅助细分为三类：**学习型**（索取信息或解释）、**任务迭代型**（与 Claude 协作推进任务）以及**验证型**（请求反馈）。

Since December 2024, we’ve found that the share of directive conversations has risen sharply, from 27% to 39%. The shares of other interaction patterns (particularly learning, task iteration, and feedback loops) have fallen slightly as a result.  
自 2024 年 12 月以来，我们发现指令式对话的占比显著上升，从 27% 增至 39%；相应地，其他交互模式（尤其是学习型、任务迭代型及反馈闭环型）的占比则略有下降。

This means that for the first time, automation (49.1%) has become more common than augmentation (47%) overall.  
这意味着，自动化（49.1%）首次整体上超过了增强辅助（47%）。

One potential explanation for this is that AI is rapidly winning users’ confidence, and becoming increasingly responsible for completing sophisticated work.  
一种可能的解释是：AI 正迅速赢得用户信任，并越来越多地承担起复杂工作的完成职责。

This could be the result of improved model capabilities. (In December 2024, when we first collected data for the Economic Index, the latest version of Claude was Sonnet 3.6.)  
这或许源于模型能力的持续提升。（2024 年 12 月，我们在首次为《经济指数》收集数据时，Claude 的最新版本为 Sonnet 3.6。）

As models get better at anticipating what users want and at producing high-quality work, users are likely more willing to trust the model’s outputs at the first attempt.  
随着模型在预判用户需求和生成高质量输出方面日益精进，用户也更倾向于在首次尝试时便信任模型的输出结果。

![Graphs showing automation overtaking augmentation from our first to third Index reports.](images/anthropic-economic-index-tracking-ai_s-role-in-the-us-and-global-economy/img_007.jpg)Automation appears to be increasing over time.  
![图表显示：从首期至第三期《经济指数》报告，自动化已超越增强辅助。](images/anthropic-economic-index-tracking-ai_s-role-in-the-us-and-global-economy/img_007.jpg)自动化程度似乎正随时间推移而持续上升。

Perhaps surprisingly, in countries with higher Claude use per capita, Claude’s uses tend towards augmentation, whereas people in lower-use countries are much more likely to prefer automation.  
令人略感意外的是：在人均 Claude 使用率更高的国家，其用途更偏向增强辅助；而在使用率较低的国家，人们则明显更倾向采用自动化方式。

Controlling for the mix of tasks in question, a 1% increase in population-adjusted use of Claude is correlated with a roughly 3% reduction in automation.  
在控制所涉任务类型构成的前提下，经人口调整后的人均 Claude 使用率每提高 1%，自动化任务占比约下降 3%。

Similarly, increases in population-adjusted Claude use are associated with a shift _away_ from automation (as in the chart below), not towards.  
类似地，经人口调整后的人均 Claude 使用率上升，往往伴随着向**远离自动化**方向的转变（如下图所示），而非朝向自动化。

We’re not yet sure why this is. It could be because early adopters in each country feel more comfortable allowing Claude to automate tasks, or it could be down to other cultural and economic factors.  
目前我们尚不清楚背后的确切原因。一种可能是各国早期采用者对让 Claude 承担自动化任务更为自在；另一种可能则涉及其他文化与经济层面的因素。

![图表显示：人均 Claude 使用率越高的国家，其自动化任务占比越低。](images/anthropic-economic-index-tracking-ai_s-role-in-the-us-and-global-economy/img_008.jpg)人均 Claude 使用率较高的国家，往往更倾向于以协作方式使用 Claude。

## **Businesses**

## **企业用户**

Using the same privacy-preserving [methodology](https://www.anthropic.com/research/clio) we use for conversations on [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e), we have begun sampling interactions from a subset of Anthropic’s first-party API customers, in a first-of-its-kind analysis.3 API customers, who tend to be businesses and developers, use Claude very differently to those who access it through [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e): they pay per token, rather than a fixed monthly subscription, and can make requests through their own programs.  
我们采用了与分析 [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e) 对话时相同的、注重隐私保护的[方法论](https://www.anthropic.com/research/clio)，首次启动了一项针对 Anthropic 一部分第一方 API 客户交互行为的抽样分析。这些 API 客户多为企业与开发者，其使用 Claude 的方式与通过 [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e) 访问 Claude 的用户截然不同：他们按 token 付费，而非采用固定月度订阅制；且可通过自有程序发起请求。

These customers’ use of Claude is especially concentrated in coding and administrative tasks: 44% of the API traffic in our sample maps to computer or mathematical tasks, compared to 36% of tasks on [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e). (As it happens, around 5% of all API traffic focuses specifically on developing and evaluating AI systems.)  
这些客户对 Claude 的使用高度集中于编程与行政事务：在我们的样本中，44% 的 API 流量对应计算机或数学类任务，而 [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e) 上同类任务仅占 36%。（事实上，全部 API 流量中约有 5% 专门用于 AI 系统的开发与评估。）

This is offset by a smaller proportion of conversations related to educational occupations (4% in the API relative to 12% on [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e)), and arts and entertainment (5% relative to 8%).  
这一趋势被教育类职业相关对话（API 中占 4%，[Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e) 中占 12%）以及艺术与娱乐类对话（API 中占 5%，[Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e) 中占 8%）的比例下降所抵消。

We also find that our API customers use Claude for task automation much more often than [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e) users. 77% of our API conversations show automation patterns, of which the vast majority are directive, while just 12% show augmentation. On [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e), the split is almost even. This could have significant economic implications: in the past, the automation of tasks has been associated with large economic transitions, as well as major productivity gains.

我们还发现，与 [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e) 用户相比，我们的 API 客户更频繁地将 Claude 用于任务自动化。在我们的 API 对话中，77% 展现出自动化模式，其中绝大多数属于指令式（directive）自动化，仅有 12% 展现出增强式（augmentation）用法；而在 [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e) 上，这两类用法的比例则几乎持平。这可能带来重大的经济影响：历史上，任务自动化往往伴随着大规模的经济转型以及显著的生产力提升。

![图表显示：Claude.ai 上增强式用途占比远高于 API，而自动化用途则相反。](images/anthropic-economic-index-tracking-ai_s-role-in-the-us-and-global-economy/img_009.jpg)[Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/) 与 API 上 Claude 的增强式与自动化应用对比。

Finally, given how API use is paid for, we can also explore whether differences in the cost of tasks (caused by differences in the number of tokens they consume) affect which tasks businesses choose to “buy”. Here, we find a _positive_ correlation between price and use: higher-cost task categories tend to see more frequent use, as in the graph below. This suggests to us that fundamental model capabilities, and the economic value generated by the models, matters more to businesses than the cost of completing the task itself.

最后，鉴于 API 使用按用量计费，我们还可进一步探究：不同任务因消耗 token 数量不同而导致的成本差异，是否会影响企业对“购买”哪些任务的决策。在此，我们发现价格与使用频率之间存在_正相关关系_：成本更高的任务类别反而使用更为频繁，如下图所示。这表明，对商业用户而言，模型的基础能力及其所创造的经济价值，比完成单个任务本身的成本更为重要。

## **Conclusion**

## **结论**

The Economic Index is designed to provide an early, empirical assessment of how AI is affecting people’s jobs and the economy. What have we found so far?

经济指数旨在提供一项早期、实证性的评估，以衡量人工智能如何影响人们的工作与整体经济。截至目前，我们有哪些发现？

Across each of the measures we cover in this report, the adoption of AI appears remarkably uneven. People in higher-income countries are more likely to use Claude, more likely to seek collaboration rather than automation, and more likely to pursue a breadth of uses beyond coding. Within the US, AI use seems to be strongly influenced by the dominant industries in local economies, from technology to tourism. And businesses are more likely to entrust Claude with agency and autonomy than consumers are.

本报告所涵盖的各项指标均显示，人工智能的采用呈现出显著的不均衡性。高收入国家的用户更倾向于使用 Claude，更倾向于寻求人机协作而非单纯自动化，并且更可能将 Claude 应用于编程之外的多样化场景。在美国国内，AI 的使用情况明显受到各地主导产业（从科技到旅游）的影响；此外，企业也比普通消费者更愿意赋予 Claude 决策权与自主性。

Beyond the fact of unevenness, it’s especially notable to us that directive automation has become much more common in conversations on [Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e) over the past nine months. The nature of people’s use of Claude is evidently still being defined: we’re still collectively deciding how much confidence we have in AI tools, and how much responsibility we should give them. So far, though, it looks like we’re becoming increasingly comfortable with AI, and willing to let it work on our behalf. We’re looking forward to revisiting this analysis over time, to see where—or, indeed, _if_—users’ choices settle as AI models improve.

除不均衡性这一基本事实外，尤为引人注目的是：过去九个月中，[Claude.ai](http://claude.ai/redirect/website.v1.ad1e2a79-c5b8-42c0-955c-e4dcc00f242e) 上的对话中，指令式自动化用法显著增多。人们对 Claude 的使用方式显然仍在持续演化之中——我们仍在共同探索：究竟该对 AI 工具抱有多大的信任？又该赋予它们多大的责任？目前看来，公众对 AI 日益熟悉与接纳，也更愿意让它代为执行任务。我们期待在未来持续更新并回溯这项分析，以观察——甚至验证——随着 AI 模型不断进步，用户的选择是否会趋于稳定，或是否根本不会“固化”。
