# The next chapter for AI in the EU

# 欧盟人工智能发展的新篇章

[阅读报告（在新窗口中打开）](https://cdn.openai.com/pdf/openai-eu-economic-blueprint-jan-2026.pdf)

**Key takeaways:**

**核心要点：**

- New program to train 20,000 SME across Europe with AI skills

- 通过“OpenAI for Europe”倡议，拓展与各国政府在国家人工智能优先事项上的合作路径  

OpenAI is today launching its EU Economic Blueprint 2.0—with new EU AI usage data and a set of initiatives designed to accelerate adoption of AI across Europe to ensure people, businesses and countries seize the full opportunity of this transformative technology.

今日，OpenAI正式发布《欧盟经济蓝图2.0》——该蓝图整合了最新欧盟人工智能使用数据，并推出一系列举措，旨在加速人工智能在整个欧洲的普及与应用，确保民众、企业及各国充分把握这一变革性技术带来的全部机遇。

That includes a new program to train 20,000 SMEs across Europe with AI skills, supported by Booking.com; a €500,000 NGO grant to support EU research into youth safety and wellbeing; and more ways for governments to partner with OpenAI on national AI priorities through the OpenAI for Europe initiative.

具体举措包括：由Booking.com支持、面向欧洲各地中小企业的全新培训计划，将为20,000家中小企业提供人工智能技能培训；向非政府组织提供50万欧元资助，支持其开展关于欧洲青少年安全与福祉的研究；以及通过“OpenAI for Europe”倡议，进一步拓展各国政府在国家人工智能优先事项上与OpenAI的合作渠道。

## Ending Europe’s AI capability overhang

## 消除欧洲人工智能能力过剩现象

The Blueprint shares new data from OpenAI on Europe’s growing AI capability overhang—the gap between what frontier AI systems can do and how people, businesses and countries are using the technology. This capability overhang, ultimately, is about the opportunity for individuals, companies, and countries to participate in the Intelligence Age. Left unaddressed, it risks concentrating productivity gains in a small number of countries, sectors, and firms, while others fall behind.

《蓝图》分享了 OpenAI 关于欧洲日益扩大的人工智能能力过剩（capability overhang）的最新数据——即前沿 AI 系统实际具备的能力，与个人、企业及国家当前对这项技术的实际应用水平之间的差距。这种“能力过剩”，归根结底关乎个体、企业和国家能否真正参与“智能时代”的机遇。若不加以应对，它将导致生产力提升成果过度集中于少数国家、行业和企业，而其他主体则面临掉队风险。

Worldwide, the typical power user uses 7x more thinking capabilities—a measure of the amount of effort the model uses to respond to user queries, reflecting usage depth—than the typical user. And across more than 70 countries with the highest ChatGPT users, leading countries use 3x more thinking capabilities per person than users in lagging countries.

全球范围内，典型重度用户所调用的“思考能力”（thinking capabilities）——即模型响应用户查询时所投入的计算努力量，用以衡量使用深度——是普通用户的 7 倍。而在 ChatGPT 用户数量最多的 70 多个国家中，领先国家人均“思考能力”使用量是落后国家的 3 倍。

OpenAI’s new data reveals the EU uses 17% more thinking capabilities on average than the rest of the world. However, there are big differences between Member States, with the most intensive country using approximately 40% more thinking capabilities than the least, and nine EU countries still fall below the global average.

OpenAI 最新数据显示，欧盟整体平均“思考能力”使用量比世界其他地区高出 17%。然而，各成员国之间差异显著：使用强度最高的国家比最低的国家高出约 40%，另有九个欧盟成员国的人均使用量仍低于全球平均水平。

We see the capability overhang as the primary challenge for Europe moving forward: the countries that have a strategy to better utilize AI will be the nations that ensure that their people have the opportunity to participate in an AI global economy. The Blueprint includes recommendations for policy makers to act including the introduction of national AI-in-education frameworks, a portable AI skills accreditation scheme, and measurement of adoption and usage at national and sector levels to monitor progress.

我们认为，“能力过剩”是欧洲未来发展的首要挑战：唯有制定出更有效利用 AI 战略的国家，才能确保其民众拥有充分机会参与人工智能驱动的全球经济。《蓝图》为政策制定者提出了一系列行动建议，包括：建立国家级“AI 融入教育”框架、推行可跨机构/跨地域认可的 AI 技能认证体系，以及在国家与行业层面开展 AI 采用率与使用深度的常态化监测，以跟踪进展。

## Introducing the SME AI Accelerator

## 推出中小企业 AI 加速器（SME AI Accelerator）

Beyond recommendations to policy makers, OpenAI is also committed to doing its part to close the gap. According to [Eurostat⁠(opens in a new window)](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/statistics-explained/SEPDF/cache/106920.pdf), in 2025, AI adoption among small businesses stood at 17%, compared with 55% among large enterprises. To help close that gap, OpenAI is launching a new SME AI Accelerator in partnership with Booking.com to help 20,000 SMEs from across the economy boost their productivity and grow their businesses with AI.

除向政策制定者提供建议外，OpenAI 亦致力于切实贡献自身力量，助力弥合这一差距。据[欧盟统计局（Eurostat）⁠(在新窗口中打开)](https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/statistics-explained/SEPDF/cache/106920.pdf)数据显示，2025 年，中小企业 AI 采用率为 17%，而大型企业的采用率则高达 55%。为助力缩小这一差距，OpenAI 将携手 Booking.com 共同推出全新“中小企业 AI 加速器”项目，支持来自各行各业的 20,000 家中小企业借助 AI 提升生产力、实现业务增长。

The program will be delivered across six countries—France, Germany, Italy, Poland, Ireland, and the UK—and is open to small business owners and teams across all sectors, including those with no technical background. We will share more detail about the in-person workshops and virtual training sessions hosted on OpenAI’s free AI learning platform, [OpenAI Academy⁠(opens in a new window)](https://academy.openai.com/), in due course.

该项目将在六个国家——法国、德国、意大利、波兰、爱尔兰和英国——同步开展，面向所有行业的中小企业主及团队开放，无论是否具备技术背景均可参与。关于在线下工作坊及线上培训课程的更多详情，包括这些活动将在 OpenAI 免费 AI 学习平台[OpenAI Academy⁠(在新窗口中打开)](https://academy.openai.com/)上举办的具体安排，我们将适时公布。

## Supporting responsible AI adoption

## 支持负责任的人工智能应用

Alongside adoption levers, trust remains a precondition for AI’s success in Europe. OpenAI was the first U.S. AI lab to sign the EU’s AI Act Code of Practice and continues to invest in safety. Building trust is also essential for the next generation growing up in a world where AI is increasingly part of everyday life.

在推动 AI 应用的各项举措之外，“信任”仍是 AI 在欧洲取得成功的关键前提。OpenAI 是首家签署欧盟《人工智能法案》行为准则（AI Act Code of Practice）的美国 AI 实验室，并持续加大安全领域投入。与此同时，在一个 AI 日益融入日常生活的世界中，为下一代构建信任同样至关重要。

To strengthen collaboration between local youth organizations, independent researchers, and AI developers, OpenAI is launching a €500,000 Youth Safety Grant Program to support the broader community working on child protection, digital wellbeing, and evidence-based approaches to youth online safety. Find out more and how to apply, [here⁠](https://openai.com/index/emea-youth-and-wellbeing-grant/).

为加强本地青年组织、独立研究人员与 AI 开发者之间的协作，OpenAI 正启动一项总额达 50 万欧元的“青年安全资助计划”（Youth Safety Grant Program），以支持更广泛社群在儿童保护、数字福祉，以及基于实证的青少年网络安全等领域的相关工作。了解更多详情及申请方式，请点击[此处⁠](https://openai.com/index/emea-youth-and-wellbeing-grant/)。

## Expanding our country level partnerships in Europe

## 拓展我们在欧洲的国家级合作伙伴关系

At OpenAI, we are keen to do our part to help countries close their capability gap too. Across Europe, we have already worked with governments and partners on a broad set of AI priorities—from sovereign infrastructure initiatives in Germany and Stargate Norway, to nation-wide access to AI in education, accelerating startup, and support skill-development programs spanning Estonia and Greece through to Ireland and Slovakia.

在 OpenAI，我们热切希望为各国弥合能力差距贡献一份力量。在欧洲，我们已与多国政府及合作伙伴围绕一系列广泛的 AI 优先事项展开合作——从德国的主权基础设施项目和挪威的“星门”（Stargate Norway）计划，到面向全国教育领域的 AI 普及、初创企业加速发展，以及覆盖爱沙尼亚、希腊、爱尔兰和斯洛伐克等国的技能培养项目。

In 2026, we will expand our work through OpenAI for Europe, a regional adaptation of our OpenAI for Countries initiative—so more European governments are supported across additional policy areas as they pursue their national AI priorities. This will include new initiatives focused on education and health, AI skills training and certifications, disaster response and preparedness, cybersecurity, and startup accelerators.

2026 年，我们将通过“OpenAI for Europe”（面向欧洲的 OpenAI）进一步拓展相关工作——这是“OpenAI for Countries”（面向各国的 OpenAI）倡议在欧洲地区的本地化延伸，旨在为更多欧洲国家政府提供支持，助其在推进本国 AI 战略目标过程中覆盖更广泛的政策领域。新举措将聚焦教育与医疗、AI 技能培训与认证、灾害响应与应急准备、网络安全，以及初创企业加速器等领域。

The EU Economic Blueprint 2.0 sets out how Europe can lead in AI, and we look forward to working together to turn that ambition into impact. Read the full report [here⁠(opens in a new window)](https://cdn.openai.com/pdf/openai-eu-economic-blueprint-jan-2026.pdf).

《欧盟经济蓝图 2.0》明确了欧洲引领全球人工智能发展的路径，我们期待与各方携手合作，将这一宏伟愿景转化为切实成果。点击此处阅读完整报告 [此处⁠（在新窗口中打开）](https://cdn.openai.com/pdf/openai-eu-economic-blueprint-jan-2026.pdf)。
