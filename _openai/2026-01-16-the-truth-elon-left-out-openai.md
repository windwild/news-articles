#### Commentary

#### 评论

Elon did not think that OpenAI needed to remain solely a non-profit. As the context shows, he agreed that OpenAI needed both a non-profit and a for-profit entity—the exact structure OpenAI has today, and that Elon is now suing OpenAI over. At the time, he said only that the non-profit should continue to exist “in some form.” Ilya, not Elon, suggested that the for-profit should have a connection to the OpenAI mission. Shortly after this call, Elon actually [created⁠](https://openai.com/index/elon-musk-wanted-an-openai-for-profit/#september-2017-elon-created-the-public-benefit-corporation-called-open-artificial-intelligence-technologies-inc) an OpenAI PBC (or “B-corp”).

埃隆并不认为OpenAI必须始终保持纯粹的非营利性质。正如上下文所示，他当时认同OpenAI应同时设立非营利实体与营利实体——这恰恰是OpenAI如今采用的组织架构，而埃隆如今正就该架构起诉OpenAI。彼时，他仅表示非营利部分应以“某种形式”继续存在。反而是伊利亚（而非埃隆）提出：营利性实体应与OpenAI的使命保持关联。就在本次通话后不久，埃隆实际上便[创立了⁠](https://openai.com/index/elon-musk-wanted-an-openai-for-profit/#september-2017-elon-created-the-public-benefit-corporation-called-open-artificial-intelligence-technologies-inc)一家名为“Open Artificial Intelligence Technologies Inc.”的OpenAI共益企业（PBC，即“B-corp”）。

Despite his court filings glossing over the details of these negotiations, they were quite intense and involved deeply personal conversations. Elon said he wanted to accumulate $80B for a self-sustaining city on Mars, and that he needed and deserved majority equity. He said that he needed full control since he’d been burned by not having it in the past, and when we discussed succession he surprised us by talking about his children controlling AGI.

尽管埃隆在法庭诉状中刻意淡化了这些谈判的细节，但实际过程极为激烈，且包含大量高度私密的对话。埃隆表示，他希望筹集800亿美元，用于在火星上建立一座可自维持的城市；他还强调自己需要并理应持有控股权。他解释称，自己必须掌握完全控制权——因为过去曾因缺乏控制权而遭受重创；而在讨论接班人问题时，他更出人意料地提出，未来应由他的子女掌控通用人工智能（AGI）。

Discussions about OpenAI’s structure—and Elon’s proposal that he lead and control it—progressed to the point that Elon asked us to check references with people who had worked closely with him. One pointed out the parallel to Elon’s Mars ambitions, which had started as a [philanthropic project⁠(opens in a new window)](https://spaceref.com/status-report/marsnow-19-profile-elon-musk-life-to-mars-foundation/) and grew into a commercial one, and mentioned that Elon tends to vilify people who quit his companies.

关于OpenAI组织架构的讨论——以及埃隆提出由其本人主导并掌控OpenAI的提议——已深入到埃隆要求我们向曾与其密切共事的人士进行背景调查的程度。其中一位人士指出，埃隆的火星计划与此类似：最初是一项[慈善项目⁠(在新窗口中打开)](https://spaceref.com/status-report/marsnow-19-profile-elon-musk-life-to-mars-foundation/)，后来逐步演变为一项商业事业；他还提到，埃隆往往会对离开其公司的人士加以贬损。

Even before negotiations over OpenAI’s structure began, Elon leveraged OpenAI for the benefit of his for-profit ventures. For example, in early 2017, he asked OpenAI to send a team to help fix Tesla’s self-driving program, Autopilot. Scott Gray, Ilya, Greg, and Andrej Karpathy ended up devoting significant time to improving Autopilot, which led to Elon recruiting Andrej to join Tesla fulltime. Elon never truly treated OpenAI as an independent non-profit.

甚至在围绕OpenAI组织架构的谈判正式开始之前，埃隆便已将OpenAI用作其营利性业务的资源杠杆。例如，早在2017年初，他就要求OpenAI派出一支团队，协助修复特斯拉的自动驾驶系统Autopilot。斯科特·格雷（Scott Gray）、伊利亚（Ilya）、格雷格（Greg）以及安德烈·卡帕西（Andrej Karpathy）为此投入了大量时间改进Autopilot，最终促使埃隆将卡帕西全职招募至特斯拉。埃隆从未真正将OpenAI视作一个独立的非营利组织。

#### Commentary  
#### 评论分析  

While Greg and Ilya preferred the B-corp, they were open to pursuing the mission within the standalone non-profit—if it could raise enough funding. Their preference was not a secret; the thinking was shared with Elon and his key staff.  
尽管格雷格与伊利亚更倾向B公司架构，但他们也表示：只要该独立非营利组织能够募集到足够资金，他们仍愿意在其框架下推进使命。这一倾向并非秘密；相关思路早已向埃隆及其核心团队坦诚沟通。

Negotiations over an OpenAI for-profit with Elon fell through in September 2017 because we wouldn’t give him absolute control. In early October, Shivon Zilis (at the time Elon’s liaison to OpenAI) told Greg and Ilya that Elon wanted them to “commit” to the non-profit by giving him two more board seats, agreeing not to quit for some period of time, and signing a non-solicit. Greg and Ilya considered this proposal but **never agreed** to it given their concerns about the non-profit’s ability to raise enough capital and Elon’s ability to start a competitor without consequence. They discussed with Shivon whether a B-corp with Elon was still possible; she said Elon had told her it could be but not for the next 6–9 months—enough time to get some distance from these intense negotiations and for Elon to focus on Tesla’s Model 3 ramp-up.  
2017年9月，OpenAI与埃隆就设立营利性实体的谈判最终破裂，原因在于我方拒绝向其授予绝对控制权。同年10月初，希冯·齐利斯（当时担任埃隆与OpenAI之间的联络人）向格雷格与伊利亚转达：埃隆希望他们以三项条件“承诺”加入该非营利组织——即额外向其授予两个董事会席位、约定在一定期限内不辞职，并签署不招揽协议。格雷格与伊利亚审慎评估了该提议，但**始终未予同意**；其顾虑主要在于：该非营利组织能否成功募集充足资金，以及埃隆若另起炉灶创办竞争对手，是否会面临任何实质制约。他们随后与希冯探讨：与埃隆合作设立B公司是否仍有余地？希冯回应称，埃隆确曾表示该选项“可行”，但至少需推迟6至9个月——此举既可为当前高度紧张的谈判留出缓冲距离，亦能让埃隆集中精力应对特斯拉Model 3的大规模量产爬坡。

A few weeks later, Elon told us that it was a happy accident that the for-profit hadn’t materialized as it never could have obtained enough funding.  

几周后，埃隆告诉我们，营利性实体未能成立实属“幸运的意外”，因为它根本无法获得足够的资金支持。

Instead, he began trying to convince us that merging OpenAI into Tesla was the only path to the mission, and it seemed that he would pursue an AGI competitor within Tesla regardless.

#### Commentary  
#### 评论

In this entry, Greg and Ilya were still considering Elon’s demand that they “commit” to the non-profit by accepting his terms—something that, again, **never happened**. There was a chance that Elon would resume funding if they agreed to his conditions; there was a chance he’d resign to go create an AGI competitor within Tesla. Greg and Ilya felt that it’d be deceptive to accept Elon’s terms and then later create a for-profit without his approval. So they were nervous that, by accepting, they’d get stuck in a structure that was unable to raise sufficient capital while Elon left to pursue his own AGI project at Tesla. **That’s why they decided not to accept his proposal**. No one ever lied to Elon; no one ever told him they’d accepted his terms when they hadn’t.  
在此条记录中，Greg 和 Ilya 仍在权衡埃隆提出的“承诺加入非营利组织”的要求——即接受他的条件；而此事，再次强调，**从未发生过**。若他们同意其条件，埃隆有可能恢复资助；但也存在另一种可能：他将辞职，转而在特斯拉内部创建一家通用人工智能（AGI）竞争对手公司。Greg 和 Ilya 认为，若先接受埃隆的条款、之后再未经其许可另行创办营利性实体，这种做法有失诚信。因此，他们担心一旦接受，便会陷入一种无法募集足够资金的僵化架构，而埃隆则抽身离开，专注于在特斯拉推进自己的 AGI 项目。**正因如此，他们最终决定拒绝该提议**。无人曾对埃隆撒谎；也无人曾向他谎称已接受其条款，而实际上并未接受。

In early November, we privately considered removing Elon from our board. We didn’t want to merge OpenAI into Tesla as he’d suggested, and he seemed intent on starting a competitor there regardless of what we did. Elon’s departure from OpenAI would have made it easier to figure out some structure for raising capital because it would have removed the impasse caused by his need for absolute control.  
11 月初，我们曾私下考虑将埃隆从董事会中除名。我们并不希望按他所建议的那样，将 OpenAI 并入特斯拉；而无论我们如何应对，他似乎都执意要在特斯拉内部另起炉灶、打造一家竞争性公司。倘若埃隆退出 OpenAI，我们将更易设计出某种可行的融资架构，因为这将消除其坚持绝对控制权所造成的僵局。

Still, though we felt Elon’s departure would have facilitated pursuit of the mission, we didn’t remove him. We kept trying to find a path forward with him because we thought that was the moral thing to do (per the same journal entry: “ **Ilya feeling like we morally should not be kicking elon out, and should be trying to make the non-profit work and convince him to stay**”). We decided to try fundraising in the non-profit for a time to see what was achievable. We said that we’d need to eventually raise $10B, which Elon said seemed totally impossible. It was clear we’d need a long-term solution other than remaining solely a non-profit indefinitely.  
然而，尽管我们认为埃隆的离开将有助于使命的推进，我们仍未将其除名。我们持续尝试与他共同寻找一条前进之路，因为我们认为这才是合乎道义的选择（同一篇日记中写道：“**Ilya 觉得，从道义上讲，我们不该将埃隆踢出局，而应努力让这家非营利组织运转起来，并说服他留下**”）。我们决定先以非营利形式开展一段时间的融资工作，以探明实际可达成的目标。我们表示，最终需筹集 100 亿美元资金，而埃隆则称这“完全不可能”。显然，我们必须寻求一种长期解决方案，而非无限期地固守纯非营利模式。

## What happened next  
## 后续发展

Over the next few months, we worked on fundraising and brainstorming ideas to obtain the next level of funding necessary to pursue and achieve the mission. In mid-January 2018, Elon congratulated us on a successful fundraise, agreed we should do an initial coin offering (“ICO”) to raise $10B—which would involve a for-profit subsidiary—and told us that we'd solved the long-term funding problem. By the end of January, however, he told us he no longer supported the ICO (which we’d soured on too by then) and that OpenAI was “on a path of certain failure relative to Google.”  
此后数月间，我们一方面推进融资工作，一方面集思广益，探索获取下一轮关键资金的可行路径，以支撑并最终实现我们的使命。2018 年 1 月中旬，埃隆就我们成功完成一轮融资向我们表示祝贺，并同意我们应通过首次代币发行（“ICO”）筹集 100 亿美元资金——该方案将设立一家营利性子公司——并称我们已“解决了长期融资难题”。但到了 1 月底，他却告知我们：他不再支持 ICO 方案（彼时我们自身对该方案也已失去兴趣），并断言 OpenAI“相对于谷歌而言，注定走向失败”。

By February 2018, Elon had decided that OpenAI would not be able to raise sufficient funding. That month, he quit even though we [told him⁠](https://openai.com/index/elon-musk-wanted-an-openai-for-profit/#january-2018-elon-said-openai-was-on-a-path-for-certain-failure-unless-we-merged-into-tesla) that we’d “ **been working on a fundraising structure that does not rely on a public offering, and we will be curious to hear your feedback**.” On Elon’s way out, he said that he supported us pursuing the path we saw to raising billions of dollars—he just didn’t think we could succeed and he would instead focus on building AGI at Tesla for that reason.  
至 2018 年 2 月，埃隆已认定 OpenAI 无法募集到充足资金。当月，他宣布退出，尽管我们此前已[明确告知他](https://openai.com/index/elon-musk-wanted-an-openai-for-profit/#january-2018-elon-said-openai-was-on-a-path-for-certain-failure-unless-we-merged-into-tesla)：“**我们正在设计一种不依赖公开募股的融资架构，非常期待听取您的反馈意见**。” 埃隆离任之际表示，他支持我们自主探索千亿级美元融资路径；但他本人并不相信我们能够成功，因此将转而专注于在特斯拉构建 AGI。

For a time, the split with Elon seemed amicable. Shivon Zilis joined our board and helped us navigate Elon’s moods when, on occasion, he lashed out. But then Elon prepared to launch xAI and Shivon stepped off our board. Elon began the campaign of harassment that he’s waging now, with relentless public attacks and various lawsuits, as he’s tried to gain competitive ground for xAI.  
一度，与埃隆的分道扬镳看似平和友好。Shivon Zilis 加入了我们的董事会，在埃隆偶有情绪爆发、激烈抨击时，协助我们妥善应对。但随后，埃隆着手筹备推出 xAI，Shivon 亦随之退出我方董事会。自此，埃隆开启了当前这场持续不断的骚扰行动：通过连绵不绝的公开攻击及多起诉讼，竭力为 xAI 谋取竞争优势。

While there’s a long way to go, we’ve made much more progress than Elon thought possible—including getting the OpenAI non-profit, now called the [OpenAI Foundation⁠](https://openai.com/foundation/), on track to become the best resourced non-profit in history.  

尽管前路依然漫长，但我们所取得的进展远超埃隆·马斯克当初的预期——包括推动原 OpenAI 非营利组织（现更名为[OpenAI 基金会⁠](https://openai.com/foundation/)）步入正轨，有望成为人类历史上资源最雄厚的非营利组织。

We’re grateful for the many users and partners who work with us to collectively advance our mission of ensuring AGI benefits all of humanity.  

我们衷心感谢众多用户与合作伙伴，正是大家携手并肩、通力协作，共同推进我们的使命：确保通用人工智能（AGI）惠及全人类。
