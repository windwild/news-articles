# How countries can end the capability overhang

# 各国如何消除能力悬差

By George Osborne, Head of OpenAI for Countries

作者：乔治·奥斯本（George Osborne），OpenAI 国家事务负责人

[Read the report(opens in a new window)](https://cdn.openai.com/pdf/openai-ending-the-capability-overhang.pdf)

[阅读报告（在新窗口中打开）](https://cdn.openai.com/pdf/openai-ending-the-capability-overhang.pdf)

AI is advancing at extraordinary speed, but many countries are not yet leveraging its full potential to benefit people and drive economic growth. There is a [capability overhang](https://openai.com/index/ai-for-self-empowerment/) between those who are taking advantage of these tools and everybody else. If that overhang continues to grow, a small number of countries will pull further ahead economically and technologically, while others risk falling behind in ways that will be difficult to reverse.

人工智能正以惊人的速度发展，但许多国家尚未充分释放其潜力，以造福民众并推动经济增长。在那些已积极采用这些工具的群体与其余所有人之间，存在一种[能力悬差（capability overhang）](https://openai.com/index/ai-for-self-empowerment/)。若这一悬差持续扩大，少数国家将在经济与技术上进一步领先，而其他国家则可能陷入难以逆转的落后境地。

New research we’re releasing today in our report, [_Ending the Capability Overhang_ ⁠(opens in a new window)](https://cdn.openai.com/pdf/openai-ending-the-capability-overhang.pdf), shows how large this overhang has already become. The typical power user relies on about seven times more advanced “thinking capabilities” than the typical user—using AI for more complex, multi-step work rather than simple prompts.

我们今日发布的报告——[_《消除能力悬差》_（在新窗口中打开）](https://cdn.openai.com/pdf/openai-ending-the-capability-overhang.pdf)——揭示了这一悬差目前已达到何等规模。典型重度用户所依赖的高级“思维能力”约为普通用户的七倍：他们利用 AI 完成更复杂、多步骤的任务，而非仅执行简单提示。

We also observe a clear country-level gap, and it isn’t driven by income alone. Across more than 70 countries with the highest ChatGPT usage, some countries use 3× more thinking capabilities per person than others. While large economies like the United States and India lead in total users—and smaller high-income countries like Singapore and the Netherlands stand out in population penetration—advanced AI adoption is not confined to wealthy nations. Countries such as Vietnam and Pakistan rank among the world’s top users of agentic tools, with more than 2× higher per-person use of advanced tasks like data analysis, Connectors, and Codex.

我们还观察到明显的国家层面差距，而这一差距并非仅由收入水平决定。在 ChatGPT 使用量最高的 70 多个国家中，部分国家人均“思维能力”使用量是另一些国家的三倍。尽管美国和印度等大型经济体在用户总数上居于领先地位，新加坡和荷兰等人口较少但高收入国家则在人均普及率方面表现突出；然而，先进 AI 的采用并不局限于富裕国家。越南和巴基斯坦等国位列全球“智能体工具（agentic tools）”使用率最高的国家之列，在数据分析、Connectors 和 Codex 等高级任务上的人均使用量高出全球平均水平两倍以上。

Put plainly, this means some countries are already using AI to solve harder problems and move faster, regardless of how many resources they have. These differences already translate into real productivity gains, freeing people to take on harder tasks, build new products and services, and accelerate innovation in ways that drive economic growth and improve living standards.

简而言之，这意味着某些国家已开始借助 AI 解决更复杂的难题，并实现更快速的发展——而这与其所拥有的资源多寡并无必然关联。这些差异已切实转化为真实可观的生产力提升：人们得以从常规任务中解放出来，承担更具挑战性的工作，开发新产品与服务，并以前所未有的速度推进创新，从而驱动经济增长、提升生活品质。

That’s why we launched [OpenAI for Countries](https://openai.com/global-affairs/openai-for-countries/) last year: to help governments and institutions put AI and its gains into the hands of more people.  

因此，我们于去年推出了[“OpenAI for Countries”（面向各国的 OpenAI）](https://openai.com/global-affairs/openai-for-countries/)：旨在帮助各国政府与机构将人工智能及其发展成果惠及更广泛的人群。

The initiative supports countries as they move from basic use to deeper adoption—including integrating AI into education systems, workplaces, and public services in ways that raise productivity, strengthen institutions, and expand opportunity. Rather than a one-size-fits-all approach, OpenAI for Countries is built around partnerships that reflect local needs, priorities, and capacity.

该倡议支持各国从初步应用迈向深度采纳——包括以提升生产力、强化制度能力、拓展发展机遇为目标，将人工智能融入教育体系、工作场所和公共服务之中。不同于“一刀切”的模式，“OpenAI for Countries”以伙伴关系为核心，紧密契合各国本地的实际需求、优先事项与发展能力。

Today at our OpenAI event alongside the World Economic Forum, we announced that we’re expanding this work in 2026 with new initiatives focused on education, health, AI skills training and certifications, disaster response and preparedness, cybersecurity, and start-up accelerators. They give nations a range of options for how to work with us to address their needs and priorities **.**

今天，在达沃斯举行的世界经济论坛期间，我们在 OpenAI 专场活动上宣布：将于 2026 年进一步拓展“OpenAI for Countries”工作，推出聚焦教育、医疗健康、人工智能技能培养与认证、灾害响应与防灾减灾、网络安全以及初创企业加速器等六大领域的新举措。这些举措为各国提供了多样化合作路径，助力其应对自身关键需求与优先事项**。**

A key focus of this expansion is helping partner nations prepare for an AI-driven world, starting with [OpenAI’s Education for Countries](https://openai.com/index/edu-for-countries/) program. It’s designed to help governments bring AI into their education systems in ways that strengthen learning and prepare students for the jobs of the future—and to work hand-in-hand with governments to improve our models and education tools. Looking forward, I’m also excited about the prospect of finding ways to work with the creative and cultural sectors of OpenAI for Countries partners.

此次拓展的关键重点之一，是协助合作伙伴国家为人工智能驱动的未来做好准备——首当其冲便是启动[“OpenAI 教育赋能各国”（Education for Countries）](https://openai.com/index/edu-for-countries/)项目。该项目旨在支持各国政府将人工智能系统性地融入本国教育体系，从而增强学习效果，并帮助学生为未来就业做好准备；同时，我们也期待与各国政府携手合作，持续优化我们的模型与教育工具。展望未来，我也十分期待探索与“OpenAI for Countries”合作伙伴国中创意产业及文化领域的协作新路径。

With more workplaces adopting AI and more employers seeking workers with AI skills, governments are increasingly treating the technology as essential education infrastructure. That means helping learners build AI skills while equipping educators with new tools and training to guide students to use the tools in ways that advance learning and critical thinking. The first set of Edu for Countries partners includes Estonia, the United Arab Emirates, Greece, Jordan, Slovakia, Kazakhstan, Trinidad & Tobago, and Italy’s CRUI. Working with ministries, universities, and researchers across these systems, the program will combine expanded access to advanced AI tools, large-scale research into AI’s impact on education and learning, training and certifications for both students and educators, and a growing global community of partners working to shape responsible approaches to using AI in education.

随着越来越多的工作场所采用人工智能、越来越多雇主寻求具备 AI 技能的人才，各国政府正日益将人工智能技术视为不可或缺的教育基础设施。这意味着：一方面要助力学习者掌握人工智能技能，另一方面也要为教育工作者配备全新工具与专业培训，引导学生以促进学习成效与批判性思维的方式使用这些工具。“教育赋能各国”首批合作伙伴包括爱沙尼亚、阿拉伯联合酋长国、希腊、约旦、斯洛伐克、哈萨克斯坦、特立尼达和多巴哥，以及意大利大学校长会议（CRUI）。本项目将与上述国家的教育部、高校及研究人员通力协作，整合四大支柱：扩大对先进 AI 工具的获取权限；开展大规模研究，深入评估人工智能对教育与学习的影响；面向学生与教师提供系统化培训与资质认证；并持续壮大一个致力于构建负责任人工智能教育实践的全球合作伙伴网络。

Like Education for Countries, the rest of our new initiatives are designed to be flexible and shaped through ongoing discussions with our partners about how to translate AI capability into real-world impact. Countries have a significant opportunity to capture productivity gains by improving adoption—scaling enterprise use, building AI-ready infrastructure, and increasing AI fluency across workforces and classrooms. And as AI capabilities continue to advance, acting now gives countries the opportunity to turn that progress into concrete benefits for people everywhere.

与“教育赋能各国”项目一样，其余各项新举措同样秉持高度灵活性原则，其具体设计与实施路径均将通过与合作伙伴持续深入的对话协商确定，确保人工智能能力切实转化为现实世界的积极影响。各国拥有重大机遇——通过提升人工智能采纳水平，释放可观的生产力红利：推动企业级规模化应用、建设人工智能就绪型基础设施、并在劳动力队伍与课堂中全面提升人工智能素养。而随着人工智能能力持续演进，当下即刻行动，将使各国得以把这一技术进步切实转化为惠及全球民众的具体福祉。
